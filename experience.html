Cedarcrest Dietetic Internship Rotations

Lehigh Valley Hospital, Cedar Crest Campus

The Lehigh Valley Hospital started my dietetic internship off with a fast-paced, acute clinical care experience. I’ve gained experience with inpatient nutrition counseling, patient charting using EPIC (an electronic health record system), many and layered disease states, and integrated healthcare.

Lutheran Home of Topton

My long-term clinical care rotation at the Lutheran Home of Topton provided experience in patient charting through Vision (an electronic health record system), pressure injury care and wound rounds, and some nutrition education.

Colonial School District

My food service management rotation with the Colonial School District allowed me to gain experience in auditing kitchens, staff meetings and education, unions, budgets, and program develop and implementation. I took on the project of finishing the development of a breakfast cart program in one of the elementary schools, which proved to be successful in increasing the school breakfast participation by close to 50% in the elementary school.

The Food Trust

I had the opportunity to intern with The Food Trust, a non-profit organization which primarily focused on food access for low income areas. I’ve gained experience in SNAP-Ed and nutrition education, grant proposal writing, program planning, and even some graphic design!

ShopRite of Hempfield

This short rotation offered a great experience in retail dietetics. I’ve gained experience with nutrition and cooking classes for groups with various needs, grocery store tours, and in-store nutrition counseling.

WIC NORTH Inc.

My community nutrition rotation with WIC NORTH Inc. in Philadelphia offered a wide variety of experiences in nutrition counseling in a low income setting, WIC benefits, group nutrition educations, food demonstrations, program planning, and some graphic design.

After collaborating with the nutritionists to assess the need of WIC participants, I created a low budget cookbook program that targets the parents or guardians, primarily mothers, with the intent of increasing the amount of cooking done in the home. Along with this cookbook, I also created and taught a lesson at a handful of WIC offices around the Philadelphia area that offered a recipe taste test, information on cooking healthy meals at home, and how to use the booklet.

St. Luke’s Rodale Institute Organic Farm

This short rotation expanded my knowledge about the food system. While at the farm, I helped prepare for the upcoming growing season by seeding, preparing growing beds, and readying the hoop houses.

10 Essential Public Health Services

Every community should take action using the 10 Essential Public Health Services as outlined by the CDC

  • Monitor health status to identify and solve community health problems
  • Diagnose and investigate health problems and health hazards in the community
  • Inform, educate, and empower people about health issues
  • Mobilize community partnerships and action to identify and solve health problems
  • Develop policies and plans that support individual and community health efforts
  • Enforce laws and regulations that protect health and ensure safety
  • Link people to needed personal health services and assure the provision of health care when otherwise unavailable
  • Assure competent public and personal health care workforce
  • Evaluate effectiveness, accessibility, and quality of personal and population-based health services

Visit the CDC website for more information about the 10 Essential Public health Services

Examples of the 10 Essential Public Health Services in action

  • WIC NORTH Inc. in Philadelphia provides supplemental government assistance to low income women, infants, and children in the area who otherwise most likely would not receive nutrition and breastfeeding counseling and assistance. WIC NORTH Inc. also provides bimonthly in-service days for all of their staff in order to keep staff moral up, maintain staff competency, and continue the education of the staff members.
  • Other exemplary public health organizations that take action in the community include Philadelphia Health Promotion Council (PHPC) and The Food Trust. (Link websites) The PHPC offers nutrition education, cooking demonstrations, education on certain diseases, as well as case management and patient navigation, all of which impose opportunities for help that may otherwise not be available to the communities of Philadelphia. PHPC also assesses the population for health needs and has a hand in health policy and systems change with the goal of strengthening the public health infrastructure. The Food Trust, another Philadelphian public health organization, doubles as a SNAP-ed agency. The Food Trust provides nutrition education classes using the SNAP-ed materials and funding to provide education in schools and community centers in low income areas. They also provide other programming focused on healthy children in schools, farmers market and corner store programming, and food buck incentive implementation. One reason they provide so many amazing services to the community is because of their extensive assessment of needs of the surrounding Philadelphia communities.

Health Promotion and Disease Prevention in Public Health

There are 3 main prevention strategies used in public health:

  1. Primary prevention, which addresses health promotion and disease prevention, usually to the general public or to a subgroup of the general public such as students. For example, in my Food Service Management rotation site, Colonial School District, they serve as many whole wheat or whole grain items as they can get away with for the students even though they have waivers which allows them to sell refined grain products.
  2. Secondary prevention entails health and disease detection which targets maybe offered to the general public, but often targets populations that are at risk. For example, one of The Food Trust programs includes blood pressure screenings through their local corner store program called Heart Smarts.
  3. Tertiary prevention focuses on disease management and disease progression prevention. One example is the diabetes education program that the Philadelphia Health Promotion council offers through their Pennsylvania Community Living Initiative. The program aims to help older adults and adults with disabilities manage their diabetes.
